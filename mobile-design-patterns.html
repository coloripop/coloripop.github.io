01 — Pages
Pages & Composition
The page is the primary canvas — what's visible in the viewport at any given state. Grids, templates, and wrapper elements define what's fixed, what floats, and what scrolls. Cultural reading conventions and information hierarchy guide placement. Users are mobile and distracted; they want information fast and easy to manipulate.

02 — Components
Components
Components are sections of a designed interactive space — as large as the viewport or appearing in front of other information. They display images, ordered data, expandable lists, and notifications, and enable primary interactions. Combined with widgets, they give the designer nearly unlimited compositional options.

03 — Widgets
Widgets
Small, reusable display and interactive elements — buttons, links, icons, indicators, tabs, tooltips. Their functions: display small amounts of related information, offer alternative views organically, provide access to controls or settings, show device state, and give quick access to indexed content. Combined with components, they offer nearly unlimited design options.

04 — Input & Output
Input & Output
User preferences depend on natural tendencies, comfort levels, and context of use. Input ranges from physical keys and handwriting to gestures and voice. Output extends beyond the screen — some users rely on an eyes-off-screen approach through haptics and audible notifications. Designers must offer interfaces that appeal to all these needs.
